# Tributary — Demo Narration Script
**Total runtime: ~3 minutes 27 seconds**
**15 scenes — pause the demo if you need more time on any scene**

---

## Scene 1: Login (0:00 – 0:15)

> "Welcome to Tributary — a professional networking platform built specifically for literacy leaders in K-12 education. Here we see the login screen. A returning user enters their email and password to access the platform. The interface is clean and purpose-built, with our converging streams logo representing how Tributary brings educators together."

---

## Scene 2: Registration (0:15 – 0:27)

> "New users can create an account in seconds. They provide their name, email, and a secure password. Registration is the first step in a guided onboarding flow that ensures every user's profile is complete and ready for meaningful connections."

---

## Scene 3: FERPA Consent (0:27 – 0:39)

> "Before accessing the platform, every user must review and accept our FERPA-compliant privacy agreement. This is critical — Tributary handles district-level demographic data, so we make it clear that no student personally identifiable information is ever collected or stored. The user reads through the policy, checks the consent box, and continues."

---

## Scene 4: District Selection (0:39 – 0:52)

> "Next, users search for and select their school district. Tributary's database is powered by NCES Common Core of Data — that's the National Center for Education Statistics. This gives us verified demographic information for every public school district in the country. The user types their district name, results appear with locale type and enrollment data, and they select the right one."

---

## Scene 5: Problem Selection (0:52 – 1:06)

> "Now comes the heart of the matching system. Users select one to three problem areas they're currently working on — things like curriculum implementation, reading assessment alignment, ELL literacy support, or data-driven instruction. These selections, combined with their district demographics, power our matching algorithm. Here Sarah selects three challenges she's facing."

---

## Scene 6: Dashboard (1:06 – 1:20)

> "After onboarding, users land on their personalized dashboard. At the top, a profile completion tracker encourages users to fill out their bio and other details. Below that, pending connection requests appear — here we see James Rivera, an 87% match, whose request Sarah accepts. The dashboard also shows a snapshot of the user's district data."

---

## Scene 7: Community Directory (1:20 – 1:36)

> "The Community Directory is where users browse and discover other literacy leaders. It shows 142 members with search, filtering by state and locale type, and sorting options. Each member card displays their name, district, a brief bio, and — most importantly — their match score. That number combines demographic similarity and shared problem areas into a single score from 0 to 100. Users can connect directly from here."

---

## Scene 8: Match Feed (1:36 – 1:51)

> "The Match Feed takes it further, showing detailed match breakdowns. For James Rivera, we see an 87 overall score — broken down into 35 points for demographic similarity and 52 points for shared problem areas. The platform explains why they matched: similar district demographics, and they both work on curriculum implementation. This transparency helps users understand the value of each connection."

---

## Scene 9: Messaging (1:51 – 2:09)

> "Once connected, users communicate through a built-in messaging system. The inbox shows conversation threads with unread badges. Here we see a real conversation between Sarah and Marcus about curriculum implementation — they're discussing CKLA piloting and LETRS training modules. Sarah types a new message to schedule a follow-up call. This is where the real peer-to-peer learning happens."

---

## Scene 10: Channels (2:09 – 2:21)

> "Channels organize the community around shared problem areas. Each channel — Curriculum Implementation, Reading Assessment, Teacher Coaching, ELL Support, Family Engagement, and Data-Driven Instruction — shows how many members are working on that challenge. Users can browse channels to discover peers beyond their direct matches."

---

## Scene 11: User Profile (2:21 – 2:33)

> "Each user has a detailed profile showing their bio, district affiliation with demographic data, and the problem areas they've selected with optional elaboration text. This gives other members the context they need to decide whether to connect — it's like a professional resume focused specifically on literacy leadership challenges."

---

## Scene 12: District Detail (2:33 – 2:45)

> "Clicking into a district reveals its full demographic profile sourced from NCES data — enrollment count, free and reduced lunch percentage, English learner percentage, and locale type. It also lists all Tributary members from that district. This data is what powers the demographic half of our matching algorithm."

---

## Scene 13: Staff Moderation (2:45 – 3:00)

> "Now we switch to the staff view. Company administrators — the 'upstream' team — have a dedicated moderation dashboard. They see platform-wide stats: total members, message volume, match acceptance rate, and user feedback scores. Staff can send direct messages, broadcast announcements, feature outstanding members, suspend users if needed, join any conversation, or delete messages. Active conversations are monitored in real time."

---

## Scene 14: Staff Analytics (3:00 – 3:15)

> "The analytics dashboard gives staff deep visibility into platform health. Member growth over time, problem area distribution across the community, message volume trends, and top district pairs by match score. All of this data can be exported to CSV for reporting. These insights help the team understand how the community is growing and engaging."

---

## Scene 15: Taxonomy Management (3:15 – 3:27)

> "Finally, staff manage the problem statement taxonomy — the categories that drive the entire matching system. They can add new problem statements, edit existing ones, assign categories, and retire outdated entries. Each statement tracks its version and how many members have selected it. This gives the upstream team full control over the matching taxonomy as literacy challenges evolve over time."

---

## Closing (speak over the end of Scene 15)

> "That's Tributary — a FERPA-compliant platform that connects literacy leaders through intelligent matching based on real district demographics and shared challenges. From onboarding to messaging to staff moderation, every feature is designed to create meaningful professional connections that improve literacy outcomes for students."

---

## Tips for Recording
- Open `demo_video_full.html` in Chrome full-screen (F11)
- Use **Pause/Play** button if you need more time on any scene
- Speak at a natural pace — the demo paces itself to give you breathing room
- Each scene has 2-4 seconds of buffer after the last animation before transitioning
- Record at 1920x1080 or 1280x800 for best quality
